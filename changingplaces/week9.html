Week 9 — International Migration and Unit Close

Syllabus focus: international migration; Australia’s urban future; unit close. Outcomes: GE5-3, GE5-4, GE5-7, GE5-8. Thread: Embracing Diversity.

Week at a glance: L25 explains the two pathways into Australia; L26 is the data lesson — pie graph of the migration intake, source countries, and the temporary skilled visa pathway; L27 closes the unit with the cloze passage, vocabulary review and reflection. Teacher: complete the program evaluation page this week.
LESSON 25

Coming to Australia

Learning intention: we are learning the two main pathways by which people migrate to Australia.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (10 min): class mind map — have you, or has someone you know, migrated to Australia from another country? Where from? (Handle with care; invite, never require, personal stories.)
  2. Teach (20 min): the two pathways, students take notes: the Migration Program (~190,000 places/year: ~130,000 skilled + ~60,000 family) and the Humanitarian Program (~14,000 places/year). Unpack the skilled occupation priority list — what skills does Australia say it wants?
  3. Main task (20 min): reading + questions on international migration to Australia.
  4. Exit ticket (5 min): name the two programs and their approximate sizes.

Main task instructions

  1. Read the international migration information and answer the structured questions.
  2. Skilled occupation list activity: students examine the government’s priority skills list and answer — which surprised you? Which would you have added? What does the list say about Australia’s needs?

External links

  • Department of Home Affairs — immi.homeaffairs.gov.au (current program numbers and skilled occupation lists)

LESSON 26

Migration by the Numbers

Learning intention: we are learning to construct a pie graph of Australia’s migration intake and interpret migration data.

Lesson outline

  1. Starter (5 min): key statistic on the board: net migration adds roughly one person to Australia every 2.5 minutes. How many during this lesson?
  2. Skills teach (10 min): constructing a pie graph — calculating degrees from percentages (×3.6).
  3. Main task (25 min): pie graph + patterns worksheet (below).
  4. Teach (10 min): source countries — historically the UK and NZ (~1.8 million overseas-born Australians), now China and India are the largest current sources. The 457/temporary skilled visa: temporary work with a pathway to permanency.
  5. Exit ticket (5 min): what proportion of the intake is humanitarian? (~7%) Did that surprise you?

Main task instructions

  1. Calculate and construct a pie graph of Australia’s migration intake: skilled ~64%, family ~29%, humanitarian ~7% (degrees: 230°, 104°, 25° — allow rounding).
  2. Complete the “Australia’s International Migration Patterns” worksheet.
  3. Reflection: 2–3 sentences on what the proportions suggest about Australia’s priorities.

External links

  • ABS migration statistics — abs.gov.au

LESSON 27

Consolidation and Unit Close

Learning intention: we are consolidating the whole unit and reflecting on how our thinking has changed.

Lesson outline

  1. Cloze (15 min): international migration cloze passage (word bank for support version).
  2. Vocabulary review (15 min): matching/Kahoot on the unit’s key terms: urbanisation, push/pull, megacity, population density, urban sprawl, heat island, internal migration, choropleth, sea/tree change, hukou, liveability, Migration Program, Humanitarian Program. (Use the student-written Kahoot questions from L4.)
  3. Reflection (15 min): “I used to think… now I think…” routine on urbanisation and migration; share a few aloud.
  4. Teacher admin: complete the program evaluation page in the PROGRAM document while impressions are fresh.

Main task instructions

  1. Complete the cloze passage and vocabulary matching.
  2. Write the “I used to think… now I think…” reflection (3–4 sentences), naming at least one specific piece of evidence from the unit that changed or confirmed their thinking.
