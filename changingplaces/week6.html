LESSON 16

Climate Graphs

Learning intention: we are learning to construct and interpret climate graphs.

Lesson outline

  1. Set-up (5 min): post the interview schedule; explain the fortnight’s routine — independent skills work while interviews run.
  2. Teach (10 min): anatomy of a climate graph: temperature as a line (left axis), rainfall as columns (right axis), months along the bottom.
  3. Independent work (40 min): textbook/Atomi climate graph exercises — construct one graph from data, interpret two others (hottest/wettest months, climate type, seasonal pattern).

Main task instructions

  1. Construct a climate graph from the supplied data table (axes labelled, correct units, line for temperature, columns for rainfall).
  2. Interpretation questions: identify maximum/minimum values, describe the seasonal pattern, suggest the climate zone.

LESSON 17

Scale and Gradient

Learning intention: we are learning to use scale to measure real distances and calculate gradient.

Lesson outline

  1. Teach (10 min): linear scale, ratio scale (1:100,000 → 1 cm = 1 km), measuring curved distances with string/paper edge; gradient = rise ÷ run.
  2. Independent work (40 min): textbook/Atomi exercises; interviews continue.
  3. Check (5 min): mark answers from the key; log completion.

Main task instructions

  1. Measure three straight-line and one curved distance on the supplied map using the scale.
  2. Calculate gradient between two marked points (rise ÷ run, expressed as 1 in x).

LESSON 18

Contour Lines

Learning intention: we are learning to read contour lines to visualise the shape of the land.

Lesson outline

  1. Teach (10 min): contour interval; close lines = steep, spread lines = gentle; ridges, valleys, spurs; drawing a simple cross-section.
  2. Independent work (40 min): textbook/Atomi contour exercises; interviews continue.
  3. Check (5 min): peer-mark from the key.

Main task instructions

  1. Identify landforms from the contour patterns on the supplied map extract.
  2. Draw a cross-section between two marked points.
